Microsoft SharePoint Storage Optimization Project

Final Summary Report

Project Overview & Inventory

In response to growing storage capacity concerns, we initiated a SharePoint Storage Optimization Project to proactively manage and sustain our environment. Our analysis revealed that we are currently using 14.4 TB of storage, with approximately 43% of SharePoint sites flagged for cleanup. The project’s goal was to reduce unnecessary storage usage, enhance performance, and avoid potential cost escalation.

Work Summary and Key Activities Performed

This has been a time-intensive and data-sensitive project, requiring significant effort and attention to detail. Below is a summary of the work completed as part of the SharePoint Storage Optimization Project, along with key findings and challenges encountered during the process.

  1. Storage Usage Analysis Across SharePoint

    Collected and analysed detailed storage data across all SharePoint sites to identify high-usage areas, versioning impact, and overall consumption trends. This helped in understanding where optimization efforts would have the most impact. Click Here to view the SharePoint Site details.

  2. Standardized Site Storage Limits

    To prevent unchecked growth, we implemented a default storage limit of 2 TB for all SharePoint sites, switching from automatic to manual allocation. A PowerShell script was developed and executed to apply these limits to all existing sites, and storage alert notifications were enabled to notify site owners at 90% capacity.

  3. Custom Script for Detailed Reporting

    Created a PowerShell script to generate an in-depth storage report, including total site size, version history size, and Preservation Hold Library (PHL) size. This provided a granular view of what's consuming storage.

  4. Identification of Inactive Sites

    Compiled a list of sites consuming 0 MB of space. A plan was initiated to contact site owners for decommissioning. A SharePoint Site Life Cycle Management policy is planned to automatically archive sites inactive for over six months.

  5. Testing the Impact of Retention Policy

    By excluding a test site from the 7-year retention policy, we observed that the Preservation Hold Library (PHL) was automatically deleted, freeing up significant space. This confirmed the retention policy is responsible for retaining data, even if versioning is disabled. This also applies to user OneDrive accounts.

  6. Versioning Configuration Optimized

    To control storage, we switched from automatic (500 versions) to a manual limit. A custom PowerShell script was developed to set the version history limit below the UI's minimum of 100. Based on InfoSec guidance, we standardized the limit to 15 versions where the retention policy allowed.

  7. Alerts and Notifications Enabled

    Threshold-based storage alerts have been enabled, ensuring site owners are notified when their site approaches 90% of the assigned quota.

  8. SharePoint Tenant Level Storage Alert Automation Using n8n

    An automated alerting system using n8n was implemented to monitor tenant-level storage. When usage exceeds 90%, it sends a detailed email alert to IT, preventing service disruptions and increasing operational visibility.

  9. Recycle Bin Monitoring Implemented

    Included periodic review and cleanup of Recycle Bin contents (both first and second stages) to reclaim additional storage space.

  10. Unused File Cleanup

    Reports were generated to identify unused or large files not accessed in the last 12 months. Owners were advised for deletion or archival.

  11. Restricted Unmanaged Site Creation

    To avoid sprawl, self-service site creation was limited, and approvals were routed through IT for better governance.

  12. Audit and Logging for Storage Trends

    Enabled auditing to track site storage growth trends, supporting ongoing capacity planning and proactive actions.

Challenges and Limitations

Retention Policy Restrictions
  • Due to the mandatory 7-year retention policy, SharePoint does not allow purging of version history or Preservation Hold Library (PHL) content during the retention period.
  • Even if versioning settings are changed at the site level, the policy overrides them, meaning old versions and deleted content are still retained.
  • This restricts the ability to fully implement certain storage-saving measures and limits the effectiveness of versioning adjustments. Clearing this data could significantly optimize storage, but doing so is not permitted due to the organization's strict data retention compliance.

Outcome

This initiative has helped optimize our SharePoint storage, reduce overhead, and bring governance into the content lifecycle. We will continue to monitor site usage and enforce best practices to maintain a healthy and sustainable SharePoint environment.
